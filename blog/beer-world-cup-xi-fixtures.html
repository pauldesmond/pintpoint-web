🇹🇷 Turkey v Paraguay 🇵🇾

Turkey 0 – 1 Paraguay on the pitch · Turkey win in the pub

A second upset of the group stage. Paraguay took the football on a tight 1-0; the Selection Committee took rather longer and reached the opposite verdict. Turkey brought Efes heritage, Bomonti's Istanbul unfiltered craft flagship, the Gara Guzu small-batch anchor and a credible Pablos/Pera/Marka modern bench. Paraguay brought four faces of Pilsen and a Brahma sub-brand. The committee noted that "Pilsen, Pilsen Premium, Pilsen Ice and Pilsen-adjacent Munich Lager" is not, technically, a squad — it is one beer with paperwork.

Pub VAR Tuborg Türk and Heineken Türkiye ruled ineligible as foreign-contract pours — same Pelforth ruling. Quilmes Paraguay and Heineken Paraguay similarly struck out. Both eligibility books opened simultaneously; the Paraguayan one closed faster only because it was shorter.

🇧🇷 Brazil v Haiti 🇭🇹

Brazil 3 – 0 Haiti on the pitch · Brazil win in the pub

Football mirrored brewing depth. Brazil's bench — Brahma and Antarctica's heritage weight, Bohemia's 1853 pedigree, Eisenbahn's German-precision craft, Bodebrown's Curitiba chocolate IPA, Wäls Petroleum's imperial stout heavyweight — is genuinely deep both ways. Haiti brought Prestige, with belief. The Selection Committee thanked Brasserie Nationale d'Haïti for the squad and noted, honestly, that one beer with conviction is still one beer.

Pub VAR Heineken Brasil and Stella Artois Brasil ruled provisionally ineligible pending contract-brewing review. Doesn't change the verdict.

🇧🇷 Brazil win

  • Roleta Russa IPA

⚖️ Pub VAR notes: Heineken Brasil, Stella Artois Brasil and Devassa all brewed locally at Heineken Araraquara — same Pelforth/Casablanca eligibility loophole, ruling pending. Caracu Stout, Brahma Extra, Dádiva, Cervejaria Avós Lager all caused arguments; the committee adjourned to a botequim and lost quorum.

🇭🇹 Haiti · provisional squad
  • Prestige — captain, vice-captain, brewer, kit-man, manager and dog
  • Prestige Cosecha Privada — anniversary special-edition reinforcement
  • Malta H — non-alcoholic, called up because Selection Committee needed numbers

⚖️ Pub VAR cleared Prestige as native despite Heineken ownership since 2011 — brewed by Brasserie Nationale d'Haïti in Port-au-Prince since 1976. The Selection Committee found six honest beers, three eligibility hearings, and no reason to pretend otherwise. Padding with rumour was declined.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Scotland v Morocco 🇲🇦

Scotland 0 – 1 Morocco on the pitch · Scotland win in the pub

Scotland conceded inside 72 seconds at Gillette Stadium and never recovered the qualification path. The Selection Committee deliberated considerably longer and reached the opposite verdict. Scotland's bench — BrewDog, Tempest, Harviestoun, Orkney, Innis & Gunn, Williams Bros and Tennent's heritage — outranged Morocco's six broadly-similar pale lagers.

Pub VAR Heineken and Pelforth Ambrée ruled ineligible as foreign-brewed contract pours.

🇺🇸 USA v Australia 🇦🇺

USA 2 – 0 Australia on the pitch · USA win in the pub

Australia had a proper squad — Coopers brought the heritage, while Mountain Culture, BentSpoke, Pirate Life and Hawkers made the selectors nervous. But against Sierra Nevada, Stone, Pliny, Two Hearted and the birthplace of the West Coast IPA, the USA take it.

Pub VAR Verdict locked. Hop legacy ruling stands.
