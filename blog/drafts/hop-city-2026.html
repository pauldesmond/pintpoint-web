Hop City pours nine

Northern Monk's flagship festival, 15–16 May 2026 at The Old Flax Store

Friday 15 and Saturday 16 May 2026, Hop City returns for its ninth year — Northern Monk's flagship festival, hosted at The Old Flax Store on Marshall Street in Holbeck, just south of Leeds city centre. Around 35 breweries from the UK, Europe and North America. Around 250 beers across the weekend. Live music, street food, natural wine and cocktails are all in the room too, but the bar is the headline and it's a serious one.

The lineup is the kind of thing that makes festival programming work: equal parts hype, depth and geography. Tripping Animals from Doral, Florida — fruited sours and pastry stouts from the part of US craft that doesn't normally cross the Atlantic. CR/AK from northern Italy, doing modern hop-forward beer better than most UK breweries. Track from Manchester, Left Handed Giant from Bristol, Thornbridge from Bakewell, Abyss from Stratford-upon-Avon — the established UK craft anchors. Floc. from Walthamstow E17, who you might have caught at the Blackhorse Beer Mile's 4th birthday a week earlier. Plus Everywhere, Glasshouse, P.i.g.s., and "many more to be announced" on the poster.

If you do beer festivals to taste breweries you can't normally reach, this is the one this year.

The Old Flax Store is one of those buildings that became a festival venue because it was already the right kind of room — high ceilings, exposed brick, the bones of the Holbeck industrial era still in the walls. Northern Monk's brewery and refectory operate out of the same complex year-round. For two days in May they hand the bar over to thirty-five breweries and let the room do what rooms like that are good for.

Sessions are split across the two days. The standard Hop City pattern in past years has been Friday evening + Saturday afternoon + Saturday evening — three distinct sessions, each with its own ticket and the ability to bin-rush a different subset of beers. Check the Northern Monk site for the 2026 session breakdown before booking.

What to drink first

The honest answer is: whatever Tripping Animals are pouring. Their stuff is on most UK festival-goer lists for a reason — they don't ship to the UK regularly, and Hop City is one of the few times you can drink them fresh on draft on this side of the Atlantic. Same logic applies to CR/AK from Italy. Get those out of the way early before the queues lengthen.

For the UK breakdown: Track's pale and IPA range is consistently among the best in the country, but their stouts deserve the festival treatment too. Left Handed Giant and Thornbridge are dependable bookends — modern UK craft and traditional English brewing done well, both at one event. Abyss work in big imperial stouts and adjunct beers; their festival-only releases tend to be the day's "what was THAT?" moments.

And the host, of course. Northern Monk's collaboration releases for Hop City are usually some of their year's better work — keep an eye on the festival-exclusive cans on the merch table for the one to take home.

Getting there from London

LNER from London King's Cross to Leeds is the obvious move — direct services, fastest around 2 hours 12 minutes. From Leeds station it's a 15-minute walk south to The Old Flax Store on Marshall Street, or a five-minute taxi if your legs aren't up for it after a Friday session. Most people stay over Friday night and get the Sunday train back; Leeds fills up around festival weekend so book accommodation early.

If you're combining it with the Blackhorse Beer Mile 4th birthday the previous Sunday (3 May), Hop City is the second weekend of UK craft in a row — Floc shows up at both, which is the sort of cross-pollination that makes the May festival circuit worth the train fares.

Why this matters

Hop City's reputation rests on programming. Other UK festivals run more breweries; some run bigger events. Hop City consistently lands a lineup that mixes Atlantic-crossing US craft, the harder-to-reach European breweries, and the UK breweries that know what they're doing — and it does it in a single venue you can walk all of in an afternoon. That programming density is what nine years of editorial discipline gets you.

Also: it's Northern Monk on home turf. The brewery's evolution over the past decade — from arch-railway start-up to one of the UK's larger independent producers — has been the backstory to a lot of the wider modern UK craft scene. Hop City is the closest thing to seeing that backstory in one room.

Around 35 breweries across two days, with around 250 beers in total. What's the connection to the Blackhorse Beer Mile?

Floc, on the Hop City lineup, is one of the breweries clustered around Walthamstow's Blackhorse Beer Mile in E17. If you missed Floc at the Mile's 4th-birthday weekend on 3 May, Hop City the following Friday-Saturday is the next chance to catch them on draft — UK craft scene crossover in real time.

If you do one beer festival in 2026, this is a strong candidate for it. Thirty-five of the world's better breweries, two days in a converted flax mill, and a programming reputation built over nine years of doing it well.
