Radio City turns seven

Saturday 9 May 2026 — Radio City Beer Works in Radley Green, just outside Chelmsford, throws its biggest birthday yet. Eleven taps, the full 7 Deadly Sins collab range, tank-fresh tasting, live music, street food, beer pong, table football, and a shuttle bus running between Radio City Social in the city centre and the brewery. Two days before the party, the same seven cans hit 35 launch venues UK-wide. This is what seven looks like.

Each of the seven beers is a collaboration with a different UK brewery — one beer per sin, brewed at Chelmsford with the partner brewer making the trip down. The line-up is the kind of curatorial company that tells you a lot about who Radio City sees themselves alongside.

Black Iris on Lust is a notable opener — Nottingham's craft anchor, ex-Sven era, the brewery behind Endless Summer and a deep run of UK indies. Brew York on Greed is the obvious crowd-pleaser. Sureshot's Envy NEIPA and Elusive's Pride West Coast bracket the IPA spectrum at opposite ends — fresh hop-forward across two distinct UK takes. Ampersand's pastry imperial for Wrath is the dessert-course beer, and Vibrant Forest's pastel sour for Gluttony is the obvious mid-session reset. A line-up that spans the styles a single drinker actually moves through across one long afternoon.

Radio City's brewery is in Radley Green — about nine miles from Chelmsford city centre, deep enough into the Essex countryside that the shuttle bus is the practical option for most. The bus runs in both directions between Radio City Social on Viaduct Road (Chelmsford station's two minutes' walk away) and the brewery itself. Book the return leg at the same time as the outbound — the brewery isn't somewhere you want to be hunting for an Uber after the eleventh tap.

This is the third birthday bash Radio City have run on-site. Previous years have sold through; this one has the can release as additional gravitational pull. If you've been to Radio City Social on a packed Saturday at the end of the Chelmsford Beer Mile, the brewery party is the same crowd, scaled up, with the people who actually brewed the beer pulling pints.

The supporting cast

A multi-beer launch this size doesn't happen without specialist supply. Radio City credit Brewers Select for the bulk supply that made seven simultaneous brews logistically possible, Simpsons Malt for the malt across the range, and BarthHaas for hops. Each collab brewer made the trip down to Chelmsford to brew on-site — Lust, Gluttony, Greed, Sloth, Wrath, Envy and Pride were all built from scratch at Radley Green, not contract-brewed and badged.

For Chelmsford readers: this isn't an isolated event. Radio City Social anchors our Chelmsford Beer Mile guide as the natural finish line of the city-centre crawl, and the brewery's range has been the engine of a craft scene that, fifteen years ago, didn't exist locally at all. Seven years on, the city has its own beer mile, a working independent brewery on the outskirts, and a 7th-birthday line-up that pulls in collaborators from Norwich, Manchester, Nottingham, York, Lewes, Hampshire and Berkshire. Not bad for a county town that lost its last historical brewery in 1927.

If you're going to the brewery

The shuttle is the answer. Radio City Social to the brewery is a 25-minute drive that's hard to do safely after a day of tasting; the bus runs both directions for £2.50 each way and the brewery emphasises booking both tickets. Tickets sell out — previous years have. The 14:05 outbound is the busiest. Get to Radio City Social early, drink a tap-fresh half there, then ride out.

For everyone else: the cans are the point. Find your nearest of the 35 first-pour venues above, get there from Thursday 7 May onwards, and run the seven in whatever order you like. Frequently asked questions

Who are Radio City Beer Works?

Radio City Beer Works is an independent craft brewery based at Old Barns Lane, Radley Green, just outside Chelmsford in Essex. Founded in 2019, the brewery's bar Radio City Social on Viaduct Road CM1 1TS is the anchor venue at the western end of the Chelmsford Beer Mile, and the brewery itself runs separately as the production site. Radio City brews across pale, hazy, sour and stout territory, with a strong line in collaborations.

What are The 7 Deadly Sins?

Seven collaboration beers brewed at Radio City to mark the brewery's 7th birthday — one beer per sin, each brewed with a different UK craft brewery: Lust with Black Iris (Nottingham), Gluttony with Vibrant Forest (Hampshire), Greed with Brew York, Sloth with Abyss (Lewes), Wrath with Ampersand (Norwich), Envy with Sureshot (Manchester), and Pride with Elusive (Berkshire).

Where can I drink the 7 Deadly Sins?

The seven cans go live at 35 first-pour launch venues UK-wide on Thursday 7 May 2026, available in cans and on tap. The full set of seven will also be on tap at the brewery's 7th Birthday Bash at Radley Green on Saturday 9 May, alongside four additional Radio City and guest taps (eleven total).

How do I get a ticket to the brewery party?

Tickets are £5 per person via rcbw.shop. A shuttle bus runs from Radio City Social in Chelmsford to the brewery in Radley Green and back, at £2.50 each way — book the COMING and GOING tickets separately to make sure you can get back. The brewery is around nine miles from the city centre, so the shuttle is the practical option for most attendees.
