The 2026 UK Beer Festival Calendar: the dates worth circling now

Five anchor dates, five different kinds of weekend. From the long-running CAMRA institutions to the new craft weekenders.

The useful question about a UK beer festival summer isn't which festivals exist. It's which kind of beer weekend are you after. The 2026 calendar is beginning to take shape — five anchor dates are already published, the split between civic CAMRA institution and big-craft spectacle is sharper than ever, and most regional festivals release dates between now and April. This is the planning guide, not the listings page.

The pattern of the early 2026 calendar is familiar to anyone who's been doing this a few years. May is the long-running CAMRA week — Cambridge, then Colchester. June starts the craft-festival run with Bristol. July is the densest weekend of the summer: Chelmsford's six-day CAMRA institution overlapping London Craft Beer Festival's all-inclusive weekend. The geography matters too — three of the five anchors are within reach of London on a single train.

Below: a calendar at a glance, then the five anchor weekends, then a short guide to which festival suits which kind of drinker. This page will be updated as more 2026 dates land.

The anchor weekends

Civic institution

Cambridge Beer Festival

18–23 May 2026
Jesus Green, Cambridge

One of the UK's longest-running and largest regional beer festivals, organised by Cambridge & District CAMRA. The Jesus Green setting — a riverside park rather than a hall — gives it the most outdoor of the big anchors, which matters in May. Cask-led, civic in temperament, treats access like the default.

Civic institution

Colchester Real Ale & Cider Festival

27–30 May 2026
Colchester Arts Centre, Essex

The 39th edition. 80-plus cask ales, 20-plus ciders & perries, plus Belgian beer, craft keg and English wine, run by CAMRA Colchester & NE Essex inside a converted Victorian church. Free entry before 3pm. Predates the gate-keeping era of UK craft and treats access accordingly.

Craft weekender

Bristol Craft Beer Festival

12–13 June 2026
Motion, Bristol

The big craft proposition outside London — same all-inclusive weekend-pass format, anchored in one of the most beer-literate cities in the country. Local brewers (Lost & Grounded, Wiper & True, Left Handed Giant, Arbor) typically anchor the lineup alongside the touring craft regulars.

Civic institution

Chelmsford Beer & Cider Festival

13–18 July 2026
Admirals Park, Chelmsford

Essex CAMRA's flagship — six days under big tents in a public park. Big cask range, deep cider list, low ticket prices, and the size to absorb a Saturday crowd without losing the local-festival feel. The default Essex anchor of the summer.

Craft weekender

London Craft Beer Festival

17–18 July 2026
Tobacco Dock, London

The biggest UK craft-festival brand. Weekend pass, unlimited pours from 70-plus breweries in 1/3 measures, music and food woven through. Closer to a craft music festival than a beer-tent event — the model the post-2010 generation of UK craft built around itself.

Which festival suits which drinker

The two formats are doing different things and it's worth being honest about that. Treat the categorisations below as orientation, not ranking — Bristol Craft and Chelmsford aren't competing for the same weekend in your head, they're competing for different drinkers.

If you want scale and a brewery line-up you've read about

London Craft Beer Festival and Bristol Craft Beer Festival. All-inclusive ticket, third-pints, the touring breweries you've seen on Instagram all pouring within walking distance of each other. Two days each — they're built to be the weekend, not part of the weekend.

If you want cask depth and a four-day pace

Cambridge, Colchester or Chelmsford. CAMRA-run, cask-led, big cider range, civic pricing. The pace is slower because the format is generous — you can dip in for a session and come back the next day. The opposite economy from the craft weekender.

If you're based in London

Three of the five anchors are within an hour by train: Cambridge (50 min from King's Cross), Chelmsford (35 min from Liverpool Street), Colchester (55 min from Liverpool Street). London Craft is on your doorstep. Bristol is the only one that genuinely asks for an overnight.

If you want a beer weekend that's also a city break

Bristol Craft Beer Festival pairs naturally with a day on the Bristol bottle-shop and taproom circuit (Small Bar, Bristol Beer Factory, Moor's tap). Cambridge does the same with a much more pub-shaped scene. Either one earns the train fare more easily than going somewhere just for the festival itself.

Still to come

The autumn calendar — GBBF in Birmingham, the regional CAMRA festivals through September and October — won't fully publish until late spring. This page will be updated as those dates land. If a regional festival you'd circle is missing here once the autumn programme is firmer, that's an editing decision, not an oversight; the brief was anchor weekends, not exhaustive coverage.

The best festival calendar is not the longest one. It's the one that helps you decide which weekends are worth protecting before summer fills up.
