The 2026 UK Beer Festival Calendar: the dates worth circling now

Six summer anchors and a year-round calendar. From the long-running CAMRA institutions to the new craft weekenders.

The useful question in a UK beer-festival year isn't which festivals exist. It's what kind of beer weekend you're actually after. The 2026 calendar is genuinely beginning to fill out — six summer anchors are already locked in, the split between civic CAMRA institution and big-craft spectacle is sharper than ever, and the autumn programme is solidifying. This is the planning guide, not the listings page.

The pattern of the 2026 calendar is familiar to anyone who's been doing this a few years. May is the long-running CAMRA week — Cambridge, then Colchester, with a small craft weekender at Maidenhead slipping into the same fortnight. June opens with regional CAMRA festivals (Thurrock, Tynedale, Norwich) and the big Bristol craft event. July is the densest weekend of the year: Chelmsford's six-day CAMRA institution overlapping London Craft Beer Festival's all-inclusive weekend. By late summer the calendar tightens again: GBBF, Leeds, St Albans and Teddington arrive in quicker succession than people tend to realise.

Below: a calendar at a glance, then the six summer anchor weekends, then the rest of the year, then a short guide to which festival suits which kind of drinker. The page is updated as more 2026 dates land.

The summer anchor weekends

Civic institution

Cambridge Beer Festival

18–23 May 2026
Jesus Green, Cambridge

One of the UK's longest-running and largest regional beer festivals, organised by Cambridge & District CAMRA. The Jesus Green setting — a riverside park rather than a hall — makes it the most open-air of the big anchors, which matters in May. Cask-led, civic in temperament, treats access like the default.

Craft weekender

Maidenhead Craft Beer Festival

23–24 May 2026
Maidenhead United FC, York Road, Maidenhead

A small, independent craft weekender from A Hoppy Place — 12 producers, 60-plus beers, cider, gin, cocktails, street food, DJs. Five minutes from the Elizabeth Line, which makes it the most accessible weekend out of London for anyone who'd otherwise drive to a county festival. Small in scale but properly curated.

Civic institution

Colchester Real Ale & Cider Festival

27–30 May 2026
Colchester Arts Centre, Essex

The 39th edition. 80-plus cask ales, 20-plus ciders & perries, plus Belgian beer, craft keg and English wine, run by CAMRA Colchester & NE Essex inside a converted Victorian church. Free entry before 3pm and a £3 ticket after — the kind of pricing that lets a four-day festival actually feel like a public event.

Craft weekender

Bristol Craft Beer Festival

12–13 June 2026
Harbourside, Bristol

The big craft proposition outside London — same all-inclusive weekend-pass format from We Are Beer (the team behind LCBF), pitched on Bristol Harbourside with the water as the visual backdrop. Local breweries (Lost & Grounded, Wiper & True, Left Handed Giant, Arbor) typically anchor the lineup alongside the touring regulars, which is what gives it more roots than the touring craft circuit usually allows.

Civic institution

Chelmsford Beer & Cider Festival

13–18 July 2026
Admirals Park, Chelmsford

Essex CAMRA's flagship — six days under big tents in a public park. Big cask range, deep cider list, low ticket prices, and the size to absorb a Saturday crowd without losing the local-festival feel. The default Essex anchor of the summer.

Craft weekender

London Craft Beer Festival

17–18 July 2026
Southwark Park, London

The biggest UK craft-festival brand, run by We Are Beer. Weekend pass, unlimited pours from 70-plus breweries in 1/3 measures, music and food woven through. Closer to a craft music festival than a beer-tent event — the model the post-2010 generation of UK craft built around itself.

The rest of the year

Beyond the six summer anchors, the calendar has another ten festivals worth a date in the diary — spring craft weekenders, mid-summer regional CAMRA events, and the autumn programme that brings GBBF, Leeds, St Albans and Teddington in tighter succession than most assume.

Spring

Craft weekender

BREW//LDN

March 2026 — dates to be confirmed
Olympia London

UK's largest craft drinks festival. 100-plus brands, expert tastings, trade-leaning programming. Historically held in early March; 2026 dates not yet published at the time of writing.

Community

Hook Beer Fest

April 2026 — dates to be confirmed
Surbiton

Community festival run by New Era Community Projects, supports local charities. 2027 already announced (16–17 April); 2026 dates likely a similar mid-April slot.

Summer (beyond the anchors)

Civic institution

Thurrock Beer & Cider Festival

2–6 June 2026
Thurrock Civic Hall, Grays, Essex

CAMRA-organised, mid-week into Saturday, sits the week before Bristol Craft. The default South Essex anchor — and one of the closest big cask festivals to East London by train, with Grays a half-hour from Fenchurch Street.

Civic institution

Tynedale Beer Festival

18–20 June 2026
Tynedale Park, Corbridge, Northumberland

25th anniversary edition. One of the North East's longest-running community festivals, held at a rugby club; "thousands of visitors" across three days. Same weekend as Bristol Craft — choose your half of the country.

Trail festival

Norwich City of Ale

Through 22 June 2026
Norwich pubs (city-wide trail)

Not a single venue: a week-long Norwich-wide pub festival where local breweries pour at participating pubs across the city. The format closest to a beer tour rather than an event.

Autumn

National flagship

Great British Beer Festival (GBBF)

August 2026 — dates to be confirmed
NEC, Birmingham

CAMRA's national flagship. 2025 ran 5–9 August at NEC Birmingham (moved from its long-time London Olympia home); 2026 dates expected to follow the same week. The largest cask event in the UK.

Craft weekender

Leeds International Beer Festival

3–6 September 2026
Kirkstall Abbey, Leeds

Four days at Kirkstall Abbey, 13th edition. Craft-led, international, with street food and live music. The setting alone — ruined 12th-century abbey — does most of the atmospheric work.

Community

Guildford Beer Festival

September 2026 — dates to be confirmed
Guildford Cricket Club, Surrey

Independent festival run by the cricket club, 15-year tradition. 2025 was 19–20 September; 2026 likely the same weekend.

Civic institution

St Albans Beer & Cider Festival

23–26 September 2026
Alban Arena, St Albans

One of the South East's larger CAMRA festivals, multi-floor at the Alban Arena, big cask + international craft range, live music, street food. The default September pick for anyone in Hertfordshire or north London.

Charity festival

Teddington Beer Festival

5–8 November 2026
Landmark Arts Centre, Teddington

70 cask ales, 18 ciders, mead, wine and no-lo — four days at a converted church benefiting the Landmark Arts Centre charity. The last anchor before winter; Bonfire Night weekend.

Which festival suits which drinker

The two main formats — craft weekender and CAMRA institution — are doing genuinely different things, and the picks below are orientation rather than ranking. Most readers won't choose one festival; they'll choose two or three across the year that match the kind of drinking they actually do.

If you want scale and a brewery line-up you've read about

London Craft Beer Festival and Bristol Craft Beer Festival, both run by We Are Beer. All-inclusive ticket, third-pints, the touring breweries you've seen on Instagram all pouring within walking distance of each other. BREW//LDN in March fills the same need at Olympia. Two days each — they're built to be the weekend, not part of the weekend.

If you want cask depth and a four-day pace

Cambridge, Colchester, Chelmsford, St Albans or Teddington — and GBBF if you can get to Birmingham. CAMRA or charity-run, cask-led, big cider range, civic pricing. The pace is slower because the format is generous: dip in for a session, come back the next day. The opposite economy from the craft weekender.

If you're based in London

Half the calendar is within an hour by train. Cambridge (50 min from King's Cross), Chelmsford (35 min from Liverpool Street), Colchester (55 min from Liverpool Street), Maidenhead (40 min via Elizabeth Line), St Albans (20 min from St Pancras), Teddington (25 min from Waterloo). LCBF at Southwark Park is a tube ride. Bristol, Tynedale and Leeds are the ones that genuinely ask for an overnight.

If you want a beer weekend that's also a city break

Bristol Craft pairs naturally with a day on the Bristol bottle-shop and taproom circuit (Small Bar, Bristol Beer Factory, Moor's tap). Leeds at Kirkstall Abbey is the same idea in the Yorkshire craft scene. Cambridge does it with a much more pub-shaped scene. Each earns the train fare more easily than going somewhere just for the festival itself.

If you want the most unusual venue

Leeds wins on atmosphere — a 12th-century ruined abbey is a hard format to beat. Colchester Arts Centre (a converted Victorian church) is the close second. Teddington's Landmark Arts Centre is another church conversion. There's a pattern here: the best UK beer festivals borrow buildings that weren't designed for them.

Still to come

Four festivals on the list have firm 2026 plans but unconfirmed dates at the time of writing: BREW//LDN (historically March), Hook Beer Fest (April), GBBF (early August), and Guildford Beer Festival (mid-September). This page is updated as those land, and other regional festivals — the autumn CAMRA branch festivals especially — will be folded in as their programmes confirm.

Know a beer festival we've missed? Let us know and we'll add it once dates are confirmed.

The best festival calendar is not the longest one. It's the one that helps you decide which weekends are worth protecting before summer fills up.
