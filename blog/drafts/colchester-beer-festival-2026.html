Colchester pours thirty-nine

80+ casks, 20+ ciders, four days at the Arts Centre — 27–30 May 2026

Wednesday 27 to Saturday 30 May 2026, the 39th Colchester Real Ale & Cider Festival takes over Colchester Arts Centre on Church Street with eighty-plus cask ales, twenty-plus real ciders and perries, plus Belgian beer, craft keg, English wine, and a no-lo selection. CAMRA Colchester & North East Essex have been running it for thirty-nine years now — that's longer than most of the breweries pouring at it have existed.

The format does what good festival programming does: stay generous about formats and quietly serious about the programming itself. Eighty cask ales is a working number — enough that there's something obscure for the geeks and something familiar for the people brought along by the geeks. The cider list is the deeper story for anyone who's been ground down by mass-market mainstream pours; twenty real ciders & perries means actual orchard pressings, not the supermarket shelf.

Free entry before 3pm every day, £3 after, CAMRA members in free at all times. The price model alone tells you what era of beer event you're at — the festival predates the gate-keeping era of UK craft and treats access like the default, not the upsell.

Colchester Arts Centre is a converted Victorian church — high ceilings, stained-glass windows, the works. As beer-festival venues go it's near the top of the UK list for sheer atmosphere. The arts centre runs music, comedy and theatre programming the rest of the year; a four-day takeover by 80 cask handpulls and 20 cider boxes is a more domestic kind of show.

Refundable glass deposit on the door. CAMRA discount on annual membership during the festival — the maths usually works out at "join CAMRA, get two free pints" if you're going to be there for more than a couple of sessions.

Where to drink in Colchester before or after

Colchester's craft scene is small but real. Any preview that skips Three Wise Monkeys and Other Monkey Brewing is missing the heart of it — the pub on Sir Isaac's Walk has been the centre of gravity for craft drinking in town for years, and the brewery in its basement (now expanded into the building next door on St Nicholas Street) is the local you'll find pouring at the festival itself.

  • Three Wise Monkeys — Sir Isaac's Walk
    Four floors: tap house, BBQ smokehouse restaurant, gin bar, basement live-music venue. The flagship of Colchester craft drinking.
  • Other Monkey Brewing — 5–6 St Nicholas Street
    Born 2016 in Three Wise Monkeys' basement, expanded 2022 into the historic Jack's building next door. Taproom + brewery; pours at the festival and the pub up the road.
  • Two Brews — Church Street
    Indie craft bar, also a 7 Deadly Sins launch venue (Radio City beers from Thu 7 May)
  • The Victoria — North Station Road
    Across from Colchester North Station, also on the Radio City Bosko launch list
  • Wooden Fender — Colchester
    Trad Greene King-flavoured pub for after-festival lower-tempo drinking

For festival-goers using PINtPOINT, opening any of those venues in the app gets you their live tap list — useful for working out whether to pre-load on a specific cask before walking up to the Arts Centre, or to chase a beer the festival's run out of.

Why this matters

The Colchester festival is one of the longer-running CAMRA events in the East — thirty-nine continuous years means it's older than the modern UK craft beer movement, and it's quietly outlasted most of the breweries it's poured. That's not nothing.

What makes 2026 specifically interesting is the breadth on the bar: alongside the cask ale that's always been there, the festival now openly programmes craft keg, Belgian beer, English wine, and no-lo. That's CAMRA leaning into the wider drinks landscape rather than gate-keeping the cask line — a small editorial move that says a lot about where festival programming has gone in the past five years.

For a four-day, £3-after-3pm, free-before-3pm deal at a venue this atmospheric: properly good value.

Frequently asked questions

When is the 39th Colchester Real Ale & Cider Festival?

Wednesday 27 to Saturday 30 May 2026, at Colchester Arts Centre, Church Street, Colchester CO1 1NF. Wed–Fri 12:00–22:30, Sat 11:00–22:30.

How much does it cost to get in?

Free admission before 3pm every day. £3 admission after 3pm. CAMRA members are free at all times. Plus a small refundable glass deposit.

What's available beyond cask ale?

80+ cask ales and 20+ real ciders and perries are the headline. Alongside those: Belgian beer, craft keg, English wine, and no-lo (low- and no-alcohol) options.

What else is in Colchester to drink at?

Three Wise Monkeys on Sir Isaac's Walk is the flagship — four floors of tap house, smokehouse restaurant, gin bar and basement live-music venue, and the centre of gravity for Colchester craft drinking. Other Monkey Brewing, the brewery born in its basement in 2016 and now around the corner at 5–6 St Nicholas Street, is the local you'll find pouring at the festival itself. Two Brews on Church Street, The Victoria opposite North Station, and Wooden Fender are also worth a stop, all on PINtPOINT with live tap-list activity. Two Brews and The Victoria are also on the Radio City Beer Works 7 Deadly Sins launch list, so will be pouring those collab beers from Thursday 7 May onwards.

If you've never done a CAMRA festival in a converted church, this is the one to do it at. Thirty-nine years of programming, eighty handpulls, and a stained-glass-windowed nave to drink it in.
