### Body prose (HTML-ready)

**Lede:**
> The team behind the Euston Tap is opening a second station-tap bar outside Liverpool Street. Soft launch Tuesday 12 May 2026, from 3pm.

**Body:**

The Whippet EC2 is the first new site from Bloomsbury Leisure Group since the original Holborn Whippet closed in 2020, and the first under the Whippet name in six years. It is also the group's first expansion beyond the Euston Tap — the tight, rail-arch beer bar at the south end of Euston Station that has been one of the quiet reference points for specialist draught in central London for the best part of a decade.

A future site at London Victoria is already being trailed.

What to expect, based on what the group has said and what their other venues have established:

- **Augustiner on tap.** One of the best-regarded Munich Helles lagers, not poured widely in the UK. Its appearance on the Whippet's line-up says more than any tap list preview could.
- **Boddingtons on cask, served with a sparkler.** A deliberate Northern-style pour for a beer that almost nowhere in London bothers to keep properly any more. Editorial, on purpose.
- **A pool table.** Pub DNA inside a craft-keg room — a move that feels closer to a Hackney taproom than a station bar.
- **Likely pizza and entertainment.** Teased from sister venues but not confirmed for the opening.

**Why this matters for central London**

Station-adjacent bars have been a soft spot on the London craft-beer map. The Euston Tap is the reference. Everything else in that genre — bar one or two — either concedes to tourist throughput or pours a thin, unambitious list. A second Bloomsbury Leisure site, inheriting the Euston Tap's curation discipline and adding space (pool, possibly food), fills a gap that has been open since the Holborn Whippet closed.

The Liverpool Street position is particularly strong. The station itself pushes heavy after-work traffic, and EC2 has been historically under-served by craft-led venues despite a dense working population in the surrounding City.

**On the "return of the Whippet" question**

The Holborn Whippet, run by the same group, was a long-running favourite among London beer drinkers until its 2020 closure. The EC2 site is not the Holborn Whippet reopened — different building, different area, different feel — but the name-continuity matters. Bloomsbury Leisure treats venues as a family, not one-offs. Whippet + Tap is their vocabulary.

**Where this goes on PINtPOINT**

The Whippet EC2 will be added to the PINtPOINT London roster ahead of launch. If the tap list stabilises in the first weeks of trading, live tap data will surface on the app's radar once we have a reliable source for the rotating half of the line-up.

For now: Tuesday 12 May, 3pm, outside Liverpool Street. Sparkler on.
